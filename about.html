After my first 4 years of experience, I decided to start with three colleagues from the previous company, a new company on our own. During this time, I was involved in the entire sales and delivery cycle. I did everything from acquiring clients and first meetings to the proposal's definition, development, and delivery.

I also deep-dived into "more serious" projects, in which I had the opportunity to develop a content management system of websites for a political party campaign in general elections, using PHP and Filemaker. And finally, in one of these developments, I had the opportunity to start using ASP.NET in its first version to create a vehicle configuration system.

New stage as a freelance and external consultant

After the stage with my own company, I continued as a freelancer, working exclusively for an agency specialized in technical advice, enterprise web development, and online marketing. I was responsible for getting the customer requirements, effort estimates, and designing the technological solution.

During this time, I was dedicated to developing web applications with the LAMP stack in sectors as diverse as travel, music, and auctions. In these projects, I used CodeIgniter and Zend PHP frameworks.

In addition to the projects in PHP, I was able to participate in the design and development of an interactive system for a multimedia information point for data capture for a wine company. In this project, I introduced the .NET stack for the first time using AJAX.

In the final stage, I was involved in the technical analysis and development of a multichannel platform for online banking services. And, as an external advisor, I was responsible for developing the restyling of a web portal for a publishing company. In this project, in addition to defining the guidelines and best practices for the multi-browser approach, I actively developed the new interface to the new corporate image using web usability standards and accessibility. Both projects used ASP.NET and MS SQL.

Wolters Kluwer

During this period my first experiences began as part of a great development team. I started at WKE through Entel (now Entelgy), and soon after, I became part of the staff. Initially, I was focused on the development of the human resources and payroll management web application restyling. The stack used was ASP.NET and MS SQL, with a strong focus on the frontend with HTML, CSS, and pure Javascript.

Then I went on, as an analyst, to perform the requirements analysis, the definition of the functional and technical analyzes of the evolutionary developments of the payroll and human resources application.

In the last stage, I was responsible in Spain for accepting and validating the architecture design of a European development platform for multi-hosting applications in WPF, Silverlight, and ASP.NET. I collaborated with the Italian and Belgian offices to migrate the existing development environment to .NET and verify that the architecture implementation met local requirements and best design practices.

During this time, I began to acquire interest and deep knowledge about design patterns, best development practices in both backend and databases, and learn about DDD, distributed systems, aspect-oriented programming, and technologies such as PostSharp, Enterprise Library, Entity Framework, Unity, Silverlight, WPF, ASP.NET and MS SQL.
