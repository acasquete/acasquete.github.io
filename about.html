My Journey: A Reflection of Purpose

Life is not just a series of moments, but a collection of milestones that shape who we are and what we strive to become. Each event, each decision, and each experience brings us closer to understanding our purpose. For me, my purpose is to explore, to grow, and to create meaningful connections—whether through my work, the people I meet, or the places I visit.

This timeline is more than a mere record of dates; it's a reflection of what I value most. It captures the passions that drive me, the relationships that have shaped me, and the milestones that have defined my personal and professional journey. By looking back at these moments, I can see the threads of purpose that run through everything I do—from the first computer I owned to the places I've traveled, and from the professional challenges I've faced to the people who have walked alongside me.

Creating a timeline like this is a powerful way to recognize what matters most in life. It reveals the choices we make and what we truly prioritize—whether it's the thrill of discovery, the pursuit of knowledge, or the importance of relationships. As I continue to move forward, this timeline reminds me of the path I've traveled and the purpose that continues to guide me.

With gratitude and purpose,
Alex

Wolters Kluwer

During this period, my first experiences began as part of a big development team. I started at WKE through Entel (now Entelgy), and soon after, I became part of the staff. Initially, I was focused on the development of the human resources and payroll management web application restyling. The stack used was ASP.NET and MS SQL, with a strong focus on the frontend with HTML, CSS, and pure Javascript.

Then I went on, as an analyst, to perform the requirements analysis, the definition of the functional and technical analyses of the evolutionary developments of the payroll and human resources application.

In the last stage, I was responsible in Spain for accepting and validating the architecture design of a European development platform for multi-hosting applications in WPF, Silverlight, and ASP.NET. I collaborated with the Italian and Belgian offices to migrate the existing development environment to .NET and verify that the architecture implementation met local requirements and best design practices.

During this time, I began to acquire interest and deep knowledge about design patterns, best development practices in both backend and databases, and learn about DDD, distributed systems, aspect-oriented programming, and technologies such as PostSharp, Enterprise Library, Entity Framework, Unity, Silverlight, WPF, ASP.NET, and MS SQL.

Freelance and external consultant

After the stage with my own company, I continued as a freelancer, working exclusively for an agency specialized in technical advice, enterprise web development, and online marketing. I was responsible for gathering customer requirements, estimating efforts, and designing the technological solution.

During this time, I was dedicated to developing web applications with the LAMP stack in sectors as diverse as travel, music, and auctions. In these projects, I used CodeIgniter and Zend PHP frameworks.

In addition to the PHP projects, I participated in the design and development of an interactive system for a multimedia information point for data capture for a wine company. In this project, I introduced the .NET stack for the first time using AJAX.

In the final stage, I was involved in the technical analysis and development of a multichannel platform for online banking services. As an external advisor, I also worked on restyling a web portal for a publishing company, developing a multi-browser interface according to web usability standards and accessibility, using ASP.NET and MS SQL.
